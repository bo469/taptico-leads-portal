The Growth Story

25 years of earned trust. A new generation ready to grow.

Grant and Leticia Willis started Willis Mechanical in 2002 with a simple idea: do the work right, show up when it counts, and treat every client like they're your only client. That philosophy turned a startup contractor into one of Atlanta's most respected commercial mechanical firms — serving data centers, hospitals, military installations, schools, and manufacturing plants for over two decades.

In 2025, something significant happened: ownership officially transitioned to 100% second-generation leadership. The next chapter of Willis Mechanical is being written right now — by people who grew up in this business and have a vision for where it goes next.

🏆 2025 Ownership Milestone

First family. Second generation. Same commitment — with new ambition. This is the kind of company that wins in a PE-dominated market when people find them. The problem is making sure people find them.

Willis Mechanical still has their very first client. That's not a marketing talking point — that's proof of something most companies in this industry can't replicate. The goal of this plan is to make sure every business in Atlanta that needs what Willis offers can find them before they call anyone else.

The Core Problem

The reputation is earned. The discoverability isn't there yet.

In commercial HVAC and mechanical services, the big players — EMCOR, Johnson Controls, ABM Industries, ACCO Brands — have national marketing budgets and dedicated digital teams. They show up everywhere online. They run retargeting ads. They publish case studies. They appear at the top of every search.

Willis Mechanical wins on reputation, relationships, and results. But in a market where facility managers, property management companies, and procurement teams are increasingly doing digital research before they make a call, being hard to find online is the same as not existing.

📉

The Discoverability Gap

25 years of excellence with limited digital footprint. New clients who don't already know Willis are not finding them. The referral network is strong — but it has a ceiling.

⚡

The Opportunity

A family-owned story in a PE-consolidation market is a massive differentiator. Healthcare and data center clients want accountability — Willis offers it. That story needs to be visible.

🏦

PE Consolidation Risk

EMCOR, ABM, and Comfort Systems are rolling up regional contractors. Every year, fewer independent commercial HVAC firms exist. Willis's independence is a feature — until it becomes a liability without the marketing to match.

🔄

Second-Gen Window

New ownership transition = new energy, new ambitions, and a fresh look at how Willis goes to market. This is the natural moment to build the marketing infrastructure the next 25 years requires.

The cost of staying invisible: Every facility manager in Atlanta who Googles "commercial HVAC contractor Atlanta" and doesn't see Willis Mechanical is a potential long-term account going to a competitor. In commercial services, a single contract can be worth $50K–$500K+ over its lifetime. The marketing investment pays for itself with one new client.
The 90-Day Growth Plan

Three phases. Built for a 25-year company entering its next chapter.

1

Days 1–30: Build the Digital Foundation

Google Business Profile audit and optimization. Local SEO for high-intent commercial search terms: "commercial HVAC contractor Atlanta," "data center cooling contractor Atlanta," "commercial plumbing Atlanta," and vertical-specific terms (healthcare HVAC, chiller maintenance, boiler service). LinkedIn company page built out with Willis's story, second-gen narrative, and client verticals. Website audit with SEO and conversion recommendations. Google Search Ads for priority verticals (data centers, healthcare) to capture in-market prospects now.

2

Days 31–60: Tell the Willis Story at Scale

Content strategy built around what makes Willis different: family ownership, second-generation transition, 25 years of reliability, and technical depth that national chains can't match. Case study development — document 2–3 landmark projects (data center, healthcare, government) that demonstrate Willis's capabilities. LinkedIn thought leadership from the second-gen ownership team. Targeted LinkedIn outreach to facility managers, property management companies, and procurement officers in Atlanta. PR pitch to Atlanta Business Chronicle and local trade publications on the second-gen ownership story.

3

Days 61–90: Build the Pipeline Engine

Email nurture sequences for prospects and past clients: preventive maintenance reminders, seasonal HVAC alerts, new service announcements, and "have you met the new team?" introduction from second-gen leadership. Referral program launch — systematize the word-of-mouth that has driven Willis for 25 years. Retargeting ads to re-engage website visitors. Commercial vertical outreach packages: targeted campaigns to data center operators, healthcare facility managers, and property management companies in metro Atlanta. Monthly reporting dashboard tracking leads, conversions, and pipeline value.
