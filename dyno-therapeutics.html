The Inflection Point

Dyno just crossed from deep research to platform company. The market doesn't know it yet.

Eric Kelsic and Adrian Veres built Dyno on a thesis that most people thought was too early: use AI to redesign the delivery systems for gene therapy, not just the genes themselves. That thesis was right — and the last 18 months proved it at a scale that changes everything.

In January 2025, Roche exercised its option on a next-generation AAV vector, signaling a potential $1B+ collaboration. In May 2025, Dyno launched the Frontiers Program, opening its AI-designed AAV platform to external developers. In November 2025, Trisk Bio became the first manufacturing partner in that program.

🚀 Three Platform Milestones in 12 Months

Roche option exercise (Jan 2025) → Frontiers Program launch (May 2025) → First manufacturing partnership (Nov 2025). This is the moment Dyno's story becomes a platform story — and platform stories need platform-level communications.

The challenge: Dyno's external communications still read like a research company. The website, LinkedIn presence, and media coverage are calibrated for scientific credibility — which matters — but they're not yet calibrated for the developer ecosystem, potential Frontiers partners, talent acquisition at scale, or the next institutional investors who will fund Series B.

The Buying Signals

Four simultaneous triggers. Each one alone is worth a call.

🔬

Roche Deal = Credibility at Scale

A $1B+ Roche collaboration is the kind of validation that converts skeptics. Most companies in Dyno's position underuse this signal. It should be visible everywhere — LinkedIn, media, partner outreach.

🌐

Frontiers Program Needs a Funnel

The Frontiers Program is a platform play — Dyno's AAV technology available to external gene therapy developers. That's a B2B product that needs inbound marketing, a clear value proposition, and a discoverable application process.

🏭

Manufacturing Partnership = Ecosystem Story

Trisk Bio as the first Frontiers manufacturing partner signals an ecosystem forming. That's a narrative worth telling publicly — to future partners, investors, and the broader gene therapy community.

🎯

Talent War in Biotech AI

Dyno competes for talent against Recursion, Insilico Medicine, and every well-funded AI biotech. The employer brand — what it means to work at Dyno right now, at this inflection point — is a recruiting advantage that most companies don't build until it's too late.
