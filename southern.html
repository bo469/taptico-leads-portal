What We Found

The Dunn's integration gap — specific findings

1

Dunn's brand is still live and driving calls to an unowned identity

Dunn's HVAC, Plumbing & Electrical still has active Google Business Profiles in Anniston and Pelham. Every inbound call, every review, every "open now" query is landing on a brand Southern now owns — but which still reads as an independent operator to the algorithm and to customers. Without consolidation, Southern can't capture the full inbound value of what they acquired.

2

Two brands in the same market = self-competition

When a prospect searches "HVAC repair Anniston AL," they may see both Dunn's and a Southern brand in the same results. Southern is now paying to split their own market share. In home services, local search visibility is a zero-sum game — every click that goes to Dunn's instead of a unified Southern brand is a click that didn't build long-term equity in the acquirer's identity.

3

The competitor window is open

Regional HVAC operators in Calhoun County and Jefferson County know Southern just acquired Dunn's. Any period of brand confusion, inconsistent messaging, or reduced review velocity is an opening for competitors to position themselves as the "local, reliable alternative" while Southern's integration is in flux. The longer the integration gap, the harder it is to recover market position.

4

Customer database overlap is unmanaged

Dunn's and any other Southern brands operating near Anniston/Pelham likely share customer addresses. Without consolidation, Southern risks double-sending campaigns, inconsistent service history, and customer confusion about who is servicing their home — a trust issue in an industry that runs entirely on repeat and referral business.

The Fix

What needs to happen in the next 30 days

NOW

Google Business Profile audit + consolidation strategy

Map every Dunn's GBP listing. Define the migration path: merge, redirect, or maintain as a Southern sub-brand with unified ownership. Protect existing review equity before it drifts.

Day 15

Local SEO deconfliction + unified keyword strategy

Eliminate cannibalization. Define which brand owns which terms in which geography. Build a single Southern brand as the dominant local search presence across Anniston and Pelham.

Day 20

Dunn's customer base re-engagement under Southern brand

Email/SMS campaign to Dunn's existing customers introducing Southern, establishing trust continuity, and setting the brand narrative: same trusted local team, now with more resources behind them.

Day 30

Competitor monitoring + market defense

Track competitor activity in Calhoun and Jefferson counties. Set up alerts for any aggressive moves into Southern's newly acquired territory during the integration window.
