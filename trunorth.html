The Growth Story

TruNorth built something real. Now they need the marketing to match.

Tony Carder and the Carder family built TruNorth Pest Control from a single-market operation into a 4-location, multi-state company in roughly five years. They did it the right way — eco-friendly practices, free inspections, community-first service, and a genuine commitment to the families and businesses they protect. In August 2025, they formally announced expansion across Georgia and Florida. New locations are opening. Hiring is active. The operation is scaling.

The problem isn't the business. The business is working. The problem is that every new market they enter, they start from zero digitally. No Google Business Profile optimized for that city. No Local Service Ads running. No local SEO content. No review velocity strategy. They show up in a new zip code and Terminix — who's been running ads in that market for 20 years — is right there waiting.

Tony Carder, CEO — August 2025: "Our expansion is about more than just growing our footprint. It is about serving families and businesses where they need us most. We understand the environments of Georgia and Florida, and we know the specific pest pressures people face here."

That's the right mission. Taptico's job is to make sure the right people can find it.

The Core Problem

The operators winning home services in 2026 aren't the biggest — they're the best-found.

Terminix has a $400M+ marketing budget. Orkin has been running Google Ads since 2003. Hawx Pest Control is PE-backed with dedicated growth teams in every market. They don't beat TruNorth on quality, on care, or on community trust. They beat them on discoverability.

When a homeowner in Warner Robins Googles "pest control near me" at 9pm on a Tuesday, they see the Google Local Service Ads at the top of the page. Those aren't TruNorth listings yet. They're Terminix. They're Hawx. They're whoever is running LSA in that zip code. The homeowner never gets to the part where TruNorth's eco-friendly, family-owned story would win them over — because they already booked the other guy.

The cost of waiting: Pest control is a recurring revenue business. The customer who books a competitor's annual plan in April stays on that plan. Every week TruNorth isn't running LSA and local SEO in each market is customer lifetime value permanently going to someone else. In home services, first-to-digital wins the market. TruNorth has the better product — they just need to be findable.
The 90-Day Growth Plan

Three phases. Four markets. One system they own forever.

1

Days 1–30: Build the Foundation Across All 4 Markets

Audit and optimize Google Business Profiles for every TruNorth location. Launch Google Local Service Ads (LSA) — the verified badge that puts TruNorth at the absolute top of local search — in Atlanta/Peachtree City, Warner Robins, Panama City, and Orlando. Build city-specific landing pages on trunorthpest.com targeting high-intent search terms: "pest control [city]," "termite treatment [city]," "mosquito control [city]." Launch a review velocity sprint — systematic outreach to past customers to build Google review count in each market. Review count and recency are the #1 LSA ranking factor.

2

Days 31–60: Launch the Brand Story in New Markets

In Florida and Middle Georgia, TruNorth is unknown. Phase 2 is brand awareness: targeted Facebook/Instagram ads in Panama City and Orlando introducing TruNorth as the eco-friendly, family-owned alternative to the national chains. Community partnership outreach to local HOAs, property managers, and commercial property owners. Social media content strategy built around Tony's story — the eco-friendly mission, the family ownership, the "we live here" angle that Terminix can never authentically tell. PR pitch to local market media about TruNorth's Georgia-to-Florida expansion story.

3

Days 61–90: Activate the Recurring Revenue Engine

Pest control's real money is in recurring plans — annual contracts, quarterly treatments, termite bonds. Phase 3 turns TruNorth's existing customer base into a retention + upsell machine. Email and SMS sequences for current customers: pre-season reminders, mosquito season alerts, termite inspection offers. Referral program launch — incentivize existing customers to refer neighbors (works especially well in HOA-heavy Atlanta suburbs). Commercial vertical content: targeted outreach to restaurants, property managers, HOAs, and healthcare facilities in each market. Analytics dashboard and monthly reporting so Tony can see exactly what the marketing is driving.
